Frequently Asked Questions (FAQS)

How are concurrent classes linked in this plan?

The concurrent classes are not directly linked but the general classes like Physics II can only be taken after completing Physics I.

What are the core level courses?

The core classes are the general classes that must be taken for completing the degree. There are many options to choose from for these classes. Please refer to UTA Degree Flowchart for details

What are the 5' 4000 level courses?

This particular star icon denotes - 5 more 4000 level classes required for your degree. You are free to choose from the given list of options. However, it is crucial to check the terms that they are being offered in as they might differ.

What are Pre-Professional and Professional Courses?

A student is only eligible to take upon 4000 level courses also known as Professional Courses after s/he has completed all of the preprofessional courses. The Pre-Professional courses include all of the 1000 level classes, few 2000 level classes(CSE 2315,Calculus II,Computer Organization) and 1 3000 level class which is CSE 3380.

How many credit hours are there in total?

A student is required to take 123 credit hours in total for the BS in Computer Science Degree Program

Do I need English I specifically for Robotics class?

The English I class might not be a direct Prerequisites for Robotics class but it is included in the pre-professional courses so you must finish that to taken not just robotics but all of the 4000 level classes. Same aplies for Physics I & II classes.

Where can I find more details?

For more details, please refer to UTA Degree Flowchart
